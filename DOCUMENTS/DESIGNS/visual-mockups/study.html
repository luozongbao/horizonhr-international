Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about studying in China

Do I need to speak Chinese to study in China? ▾
Not necessarily. Many universities offer English-taught programs, especially for international students. However, learning Mandarin will significantly improve your daily life and career prospects in China. We offer language preparation courses and HSK test support.
Are scholarships available for international students? ▾
Yes. The Chinese Government Scholarship (CSC), provincial scholarships, and university-specific scholarships are available. HBHR consultants can help you identify and apply for the scholarships you qualify for.
How long does the admission process take? ▾
Typically 4–8 weeks from document submission to receiving an admission letter. Visa processing takes an additional 2–4 weeks. We recommend starting 3–4 months before your intended start date.
What documents are required for application? ▾
Generally required: Passport copy, academic transcripts, graduation certificate, physical examination report, non-criminal record certificate, personal statement, and 2 recommendation letters. Requirements may vary by university and program.
Is accommodation provided by the university? ▾
Most partner universities provide on-campus dormitories for international students at affordable rates (typically RMB 800–2,500/month). We also assist with off-campus apartment searching in cities where needed.
